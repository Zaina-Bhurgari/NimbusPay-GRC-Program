# NimbusPay-GRC-Program
NimbusPay GRC Portfolio

## Overview

This repository simulates a full **Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) program** for a fictional fintech organization, *NimbusPay*.

The project demonstrates how an organization can identify, assess, and manage cybersecurity and operational risks while aligning with industry-recognized frameworks such as NIST 800-53 and SOC 2.

Rather than presenting isolated documents, this repository is structured to reflect how a **real-world GRC function operates**, from risk identification through control implementation and governance.

---

## Objectives

* Perform a comprehensive **enterprise risk assessment**
* Analyze **technical and application-level vulnerabilities and risks**
* Develop and recommend **security controls**
* Establish **policies and governance structures**
* Simulate a scalable **GRC program for a fintech environment**

---

## Repository Structure

### 1. Governance

Defines the policies and rules that guide security and risk management.

* Information Security Policy
* Access Control Policy
* Incident Response Policy
* Vendor Risk Management Policy
* Acceptable Use Policy

---

### 2. Risk Management

Identifies and evaluates risks across the organization.

**Enterprise Risk Assessment**

* Risk register (25 risks identified and scored)
* Risk heat map visualization
* Business, operational, and cybersecurity risk analysis

**Software Vulnerability Assessment**

* Technical risk analysis of application stack and cloud infrastructure
* Threat scenarios involving APIs, cloud services, and CI/CD pipelines

---

### 3. Controls

Defines how identified risks are mitigated.

Control areas include:

* Access Control & Identity Management
* Logging & Monitoring
* Incident Response
* Secure Development (CI/CD)
* Data Protection
* Third-Party Risk Management

Each control area is designed to address multiple risks and align with established security standards.

---

### 4. Policies

Establishes enforceable governance mechanisms to support controls.

Policies are structured to define:

* Purpose
* Scope
* Requirements
* Enforcement

These documents translate control recommendations into **organizational expectations and accountability**.

---

## Key Highlights

* Developed a **risk register of 25 risks** across technical, operational, and third-party domains
* Applied a **qualitative risk model** using:

  * Likelihood scale (1–5)
  * Non-linear impact scale (1, 2, 4, 8, 16) to reflect real-world business impact
* Designed a **risk heat map** to visualize prioritization
* Mapped controls to **NIST 800-53**
* Structured outputs to reflect **real GRC workflows and deliverables**

---

## Methodology

This project follows a combined:

* Asset-based approach (identifying critical systems and data)
* Threat-based approach (modeling realistic attack scenarios)
* Vulnerability-based approach (analyzing weaknesses in systems and processes)

Risks are evaluated based on likelihood and impact, then prioritized for mitigation through control implementation.
